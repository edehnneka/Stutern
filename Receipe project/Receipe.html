Oha(Ora) Soup

Oha soup is a Nigerian delicacy from the Eastern region, it is commonly prepared by the Igbo peopple from an evergreen tree whose botanical name is Pterocarpus mildraedii. The main ingredient for the soup is the oha leaf, others include uziza,achi (soup thickener), meat, crayfish ,palm oil and salt. It is a very traditional soup similar to the bitterleaf soup but cooked with Ora leaves. Oha Soup is special because the tender ora leaves used in preparing this soup recipe are seasonal unlike their bitterleaf counterpart which can be found all year round.


Ingredients Measurement
Oha leaves 2 bunches
Cocoyam 8 Tubers
Red palm oil 4 teaspoon
Chilli pepper 1/2 teaspoon
Stock cubes 2 cubes
Salt 1/4 teaspoon
Diced onions 1 Bulb
Ogiri (Local spice) 1/3 teaspoon
Assorted meat As pleased
Dried fish As pleased
Stock fish As pleased
Grinded crayfish 2 teaspoon
Semolina 2 cups

 

Steps/Method of preparation

  1. Wash the meat, season with stock cubes, salt, pepper, water and onions and cook until tender.
    2.  
  2. Use boiling water to wash the stockfish and cook separately in another pot. (It is optional to cook the stockfish together with the meat).
    3.  
  3. De-bone the dry fish and wash thoroughly with hot water(set aside).
    4.  
  4. Once the meat is tenderly cooked, add the red palm oil into the pot.
    5.  
  5. Pour 100ml of hot water into the powdered cocoyam, stir properly to form a thick paste.
    6.  
  6. However, if using the raw cocoyam tubers, cook tenderly and blend to form a smooth paste.
    7.  
  7. Afterwards, add the cocoyam into the pot of soup, add the remaining ingredients except the oha leaves and allow to cook until the soup has thickened to your choice.
    8.  
  8. Wash and thickly cut the oha leaves before adding into the pot of soup.
    9.  
  9. Allow to simmer for 3mins, stir and bring down from fire.
    10.  
  10. For the semolina, pour water in a clean pot and allow to boil.
    11.  
  11. Stir in the semolina into the boiling water and continue stirring until thickened.
    12.  
  12. Sprinkle hot water over it and allow to cook for another 3 mins before bringing down from fire.
    13.  
  13. Serve warm with either pounded yam, fufu or wheat.
    14.
